## 1. Stereo rectification (Week 2 #1)

**Goal.** Replace `depth.py`'s rotate-90-and-swap heuristic with full
`cv2.stereoRectify`. Spec gate: mean depth within 5 % of 0.41 m true
optical distance.

**Three nested bugs to clear.**

(a) **CV_16S disparity in `reprojectImageTo3D`.** SGBM emits fixed-point
disp ×16; reprojectImageTo3D treats CV_16S as integer (no fractional
bits) and gives Z that's 16× too small. Fix: pass `disp.astype(float)
/ 16.0`.

(b) **Vertical-baseline stereo breaks SGBM.** Cam A above Cam B in world
means `stereoRectify` correctly emits P matrices with the rectified
baseline along the Y row (P2[1,3]), not X (P2[0,3]). My
baseline-extraction was only reading P2[0,3] so it printed 0.0000 m —
but the deeper failure was that vertical-stereo rectified images have
*vertical* epipolar lines, and SGBM only matches along horizontal rows.
Fix: pre-rotate both inputs 90° CW before rectification (equivalent to
rotating the camera 90° CCW around its optical axis), with matching K
+ pose adjustments. After pre-rotation, `t_a_to_b = (-0.194, 0, +0.049)
m` — primarily horizontal, axis comes out "horizontal" from
stereoRectify, rectified baseline P2[0,3] reads 0.2000 m ✓.

(c) **`alpha=0` inflated fx_rect by 3.6×.** With alpha=0 the rectified
ROI gets cropped to the largest valid-in-both rectangle, then K_rect is
scaled to fit the original image dimensions back into that small ROI —
inflating fx from 1108 → 3955 px. At fx 3955 the chest at 0.40 m needs
disparity ~1900 px, far above the num_disp 640 cap. Fix: `alpha=-1`
(auto). fx_rect drops back to 1108.5 (matches original K), expected
chest disp 554 px, observed 579 px — within cap.

**Result.** Mean depth 0.400 m vs 0.41 m true (within 2.5 %). Mean cz
1.989 mm at 15.016 BPM (Δ +0.016). Smoke ✅.

**Considered and rejected.** Switching SGBM to MODE_SGBM (vs 3WAY) for
cheaper memory — needed at num_disp 1024+ but ran into a different
"input width too small for max disparity" hard error. Bottom line, the
post-rotation 800-px width is the binding constraint here, not the
matcher mode.

## 2. Smoke-test chest box tightened

**Symptom.** With rectification fixed, peak-pick went 30 BPM (octave
error). Centroid-method went 19.7 BPM (split between 15 and 30 power).

**Root cause.** Chest box was 24×24×40 cm — flagged in Week 1 results
doc as "3× too large in Y vs the actual ~8×30×8 cm torso". The box
pulled in mattress pixels (mattress top at shared-Y 0.10 sits inside
the lower Y bound) which don't move. As the chest deforms, the
set-membership shifts in the box have a strong 2nd harmonic from
geometric non-linearity. The 30-BPM peak was a real signal artifact,
not a peak-pick failure — same failure-mode family as task #78
(Gdańsk octave-error algorithm).

**Fix.** Box tightened to torso geometry: `half_x=0.08, half_y=0.05,
half_z=0.15`, centred on chest. Excludes mattress; covers torso middle
band. Restores 15-BPM dominance.

**Considered and rejected.** Switching peak-pick → centroid method as
the smoke method. Treats the symptom, not the cause. The 30-BPM
artifact is intrinsic to the noisy axis (see §4) and would re-appear at
larger amplitudes.

## 3. num_disp sized from measured min depth — capped at 784 by image
   width

Per the steer "don't take num_disp on faith — print min depth and size
to it":

`depth.py` now prints both scene min depth and scene max disp per run,
flags clipping when max disp hits the ceiling. Tried 1024, 1280 — both
SGBM OOM'd (cost-volume blows up). The hard cap comes from SGBM's
`image_width − (minDisp + numDisp) > block/2` constraint: after CW
pre-rotation the rectified image width is 800 px, capping
`minDisp + numDisp` at 784 for block 9.

At 784:
- Z_min resolvable: 0.283 m
- Scene min depth measured: 0.283 m (= Z_min, still clipping in part)
- Scene max disp: 783 px (1 px headroom)
- 27 % of valid pixels saturate at the cap

The 27 % saturating pixels are cot rails / cot floor close to Cam A,
not chest pixels — chest sits at depth ~0.40 m with disp ~580 (clear of
the cap). Smoke still PASSES at 15.016 BPM. Acceptable for the current
side-on geometry; re-check after #2.

**Path to raise the cap beyond 784:** render at higher resolution (e.g.
2560×1600), pre-rotated width grows to 1600 → max num_disp ~1595.
~4× SGBM cost. Defer until evidence we need it (e.g. an L-overhang
geometry with closer working distance, or a use-case that needs
sub-30-cm scene structure).

## 4. Amplitude reference reconciled — Week 1 "70 %" was the wrong
   denominator

**The trap.** Week 1 results doc framed cz_std recovery vs "true ~7 mm"
implying physical chest displacement. I extended this to "70 % of
2.83 mm (std of an 8 mm peak-to-peak sinusoid)" on Week-2 #1 results.
Both denominators were wrong: they're the std of the chest **apex**
displacement, not what the centroid signal would read under perfect
depth recovery.

**Forward-modeling the right reference.** Sampled the deformed torso
surface analytically per frame (using the synthetic shape-key value
from `ground_truth.csv` and `scene_build.py`'s shape-key drive
formula), ran the same chest_centroid_z over the synthetic cloud:

| Axis              | Ideal std | Ideal p2p | Measured std | Recovery |
|-------------------|-----------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| shared-Y (height) | 0.32 mm   | 0.90 mm   | 0.21 mm      | 66 %     |
| shared-Z (forward, current smoke) | **0.0** | **0.0** | 1.72 mm | undefined (axis-orthogonal noise) |
| Cam-A depth (sensor-centric) | 0.078 mm | 0.22 mm | 1.72 mm | **22×** over ideal |

What this means:

- The current smoke `cz` signal (shared-Z) is geometry-orthogonal to
  the breathing direction. Chest moves in shared-Y (Blender world Z);
  shared-Z doesn't change. The 1.72 mm measured is set-membership /
  quantization noise, amplitude-modulated by breathing — peak-pick
  still locks 15 BPM because of that modulation, but the std is not
  amplitude.
- The honest amplitude-recovery axis is **shared-Y**. 66 % recovery vs
  the 0.32 mm ideal centroid std is a real number — not 70 %, not
  defined against 2.83 mm.
- **Cam-A depth (sensor-centric) is 22× over ideal — noise-dominated.**
  Because the current cameras sit nearly side-on to the chest rise,
  chest motion barely projects onto the optical axis: ideal sensor
  signal std is only 0.078 mm. This is exactly what #2 (L-overhang,
  cams pitched down at the chest) is meant to fix.

**Cessation threshold (0.12 mm) — also retract the "17×".** The
headroom claim was built on the wrong denominator. The right framing:
- On shared-Y the ideal centroid signal during normal breathing is
  0.32 mm vs threshold 0.12 mm → 2.7× headroom in the ideal case
  (recovery 66 % puts it at ~1.8× measured).
- On Cam-A depth (probably what the threshold was derived against), the
  *ideal* signal is below threshold — cessation detector would
  false-positive on normal breathing in this geometry.
- Cessation validation cannot proceed cleanly until #2 lands AND we
  reconcile the threshold's signal-of-record with the simulation
  signal.

`smoke_test.py` now reports all three axes with ideal/measured/p2p/ratio
under `amplitude_recovery` in `smoke_report.json`. Gate language is
honest for the first time.
